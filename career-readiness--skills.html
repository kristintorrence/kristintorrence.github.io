Career Readiness

Skills

These are what students should know and be able to do at their Internship.

  1. Articulate professional goals, personal strengths and skills, passions, and experience in order to advocate for themselves for a role when engaging with their manager or when networking.
  2. Seek out resources and people to expand on existing knowledge and learn new skills, needed for advancement.
  3. Maintain a professional external perception and manage personal branding through in-person and online presence.

Career Readiness Skills

3D Model

  • Key Demonstrations of Training Outcomes

    In order to determine that your students have learned the content, you should provide opportunities for students to demonstrate learning in the following ways:

    1. During a mock interview, the student demonstrated the ability to market and advocate for themselves when engaging with their manager or when networking by clearly and confidently articulating professional goals, personal strengths, skills, passions, and experience. [1]
    2. When given a growth area assignment, the student sought out resources and guidance from people to learn a new or further develop a skill. [2]
    3. During a social media audit and interactions with 2+ site personnel other than one’s own coach or instructor, the student consistently presented one’s self in a professional manner. [3]
