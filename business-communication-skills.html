Business Communication

Skills

These are what students should know and be able to do at their Internship.

  1. Follow email communication etiquette and utilize email composition best practices
  2. Utilize common computer system tools and business applications for documenting, organizing, and managing information
  3. Actively and effectively participate in meetings and manage meeting responsibilities and logistics.
  4. Utilize processes to clearly articulate ideas and compose professionally written business documents.

Business Communication Skills 3D Model

  • Key Demonstrations of Training Outcomes

    In order to determine that your students have learned the content, you should provide opportunities for students to demonstrate learning in the following ways:

    1. During a professional email correspondence, the student displayed email composition norms for structure, content, hyperlinks, signature, and reference documents, and utilized common features in Outlook to support this. [1]
    2. During a professional email correspondence, the student demonstrated email communication etiquette in regards to turn around time, appropriate addressing and replying, and utilized common features in Outlook to support this. [1]
    3. When given a set of goals and requirements for an end-deliverable Business Communication assignment, the student demonstrated basic proficiency with utilizing Microsoft Word, Excel and PowerPoint for documentation and information management. [2]
    4. During a team-based project, the student was an active participant in meetings (in-person, virtual, phone) and utilized clear, concise, and confident verbal speech and active listening, reading cues and discerning appropriate times to share, question, and interject. [3]
    5. During a team-based project, the student was able to schedule a meeting, send meeting invitations, create a meeting agenda, facilitate a meeting, document meeting minutes, and management meeting logistics. [3]
    6. During a writing project, the student was able to compose multiple drafts from the ideation stage to final version, implementing feedback with every subsequent iteration. [4]
