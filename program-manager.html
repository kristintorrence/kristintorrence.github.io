Skills

These are what students should know and be able to do at their Internship.

  1. Follow email communication etiquette and utilize email composition best practices
  2. Utilize common computer system tools and business applications for documenting, organizing, and managing information
  3. Actively and effectively participate in meetings and manage meeting responsibilities and logistics.
  4. Utilize processes to clearly articulate ideas and compose professionally written business documents.

  • Key Demonstrations of Training Outcomes

    In order to determine that your students have learned the content, you should provide opportunities for students to demonstrate learning in the following ways:

    1. During a professional email correspondence, the student displayed email composition norms for structure, content, hyperlinks, signature, and reference documents, and utilized common features in Outlook to support this. [1]
    2. During a professional email correspondence, the student demonstrated email communication etiquette in regards to turn around time, appropriate addressing and replying, and utilized common features in Outlook to support this. [1]
    3. When given a set of goals and requirements for an end-deliverable Business Communication assignment, the student demonstrated basic proficiency with utilizing Microsoft Word, Excel and PowerPoint for documentation and information management. [2]
    4. During a team-based project, the student was an active participant in meetings (in-person, virtual, phone) and utilized clear, concise, and confident verbal speech and active listening, reading cues and discerning appropriate times to share, question, and interject. [3]
    5. During a team-based project, the student was able to schedule a meeting, send meeting invitations, create a meeting agenda, facilitate a meeting, document meeting minutes, and management meeting logistics. [3]
    6. During a writing project, the student was able to compose multiple drafts from the ideation stage to final version, implementing feedback with every subsequent iteration. [4]

     

     

Skills

These are what students should know and be able to do at their Internship.

  1. Articulate professional goals, personal strengths and skills, passions, and experience in order to advocate for themselves for a role when engaging with their manager or when networking.
  2. Seek out resources and people to expand on existing knowledge and learn new skills, needed for advancement.
  3. Maintain a professional external perception and manage personal branding through in-person and online presence.

 

  • Key Demonstrations of Training Outcomes

    In order to determine that your students have learned the content, you should provide opportunities for students to demonstrate learning in the following ways:

    1. During a mock interview, the student demonstrated the ability to market and advocate for themselves when engaging with their manager or when networking by clearly and confidently articulating professional goals, personal strengths, skills, passions, and experience. [1]
    2. When given a growth area assignment, the student sought out resources and guidance from people to learn a new or further develop a skill. [2]
    3. During a social media audit and interactions with 2+ site personnel other than one’s own coach or instructor, the student consistently presented one’s self in a professional manner. [3]

     

Skills

These are what students should know and be able to do at their Internship.

  1. Respect and align behavior in a team/workplace environment to ethical standards, safety concerns, and social and professional workplace norms
  2. Exert strong organization and effort in the process of planning for and executing on work deliverables.
  3. Engage in consistent communications with manager and team, as agreed upon and reliably participates as expected.
  4. Actively seeks out new ideas and solutions to bring to manager and team; anticipates and proactively communicates changes in plans and status of deliverables.
  5. Confidently initiate and tactfully engage in correspondence with others to develop new respectful, professional relationships.

  • Key Demonstrations of Training Outcomes

    In order to determine that your students have learned the content, you should provide opportunities for students to demonstrate learning in the following ways:

    1. During a team-based assignment, the student adapted behavior to team and workplace norms, including aligning to ethical standards, safety concerns, and social and professional workplace practices. [1]
    2. When given a set of goals and requirements for an end-deliverable Business Communication assignment, the student demonstrated clear understanding of the expectations, developed a plan for executing with quality, practiced organization and time-management, and exerted great effort to meet expectations and produce a high-quality in a timely fashion. [2]
    3. During a team-based meeting, the student communicated pertinent information, consistently participated in discussion, and collaborated with teammates as expected, and followed through with responsibilities and commitments due at that time. [3]
    4. During a team-based project, the student generated creative ideas and proactively communicated plans and status updates to the team. [4]
    5. During a networking event, the student proactively initiated conversation with other event attendees of whom they do not know and solidified the connection by following up personally with them over virtual means (email or LinkedIn). [5]

Skills

These are what students should know and be able to do at their Internship.

  1. Proactively seeks clear understanding of team, workplace, ethical and safety norms of the work environment and aligns behaviors and presentation accordingly.
  2. Accurately identify and regulate one’s emotions and thoughts to maintain positive composure and uphold professional etiquette with minimal prompting from staff.
  3. Proactively initiate correspondence with manager or teammates to surface issues in a tactful and constructive way, exerting an appropriate level of self-advocacy in communicating challenges, expectations, and needs, to those who can influence it.
  4. Actively seek out and apply feedback from manager, team members and clients to refine performance.
  5. Display grit (ex. effort, perseverance) when navigating difficult or challenging circumstances (personal issues, disempowering conversation, setbacks, poor performance or failure).

  • Key Demonstrations of Training Outcomes

    In order to determine that your students have learned the content, you should provide opportunities for students to demonstrate learning in the following ways:

    1. During a team-based project, the student consistently abided by team, workplace, ethical, and safety norms and professional presentation and behavior, as observed by their instructor and classmates. [1]
    2. During a difficult coaching or feedback session, the student upheld professional etiquette with minimal prompting from staff and maintained positive composure by self-regulating emotions and thoughts. [2]
    3. During a team-based assignment, the student proactively communicated challenges, expectations, team and/or individual needs, and tactfully surfaced issues with the team in efforts to ameliorate them. [3]
    4. During a team-based project, the student actively collected feedback for improvement and applied feedback to their performance. [4]
    5. During a coaching session to address challenges, setbacks, poor performance, or failure, the student demonstrated resilience, perseverance, tenacity, and unrelenting determination and effort toward improving the growth area. [5]
